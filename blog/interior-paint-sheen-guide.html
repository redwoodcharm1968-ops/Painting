How to Choose the Right Interior Paint Sheen

Color gets all the attention, but sheen is what you live with every day. Pick the wrong finish and your walls show every scuff, every brush stroke, and every bit of glare from a Duluth winter morning. Pick the right one and the room looks richer, cleans easier, and holds up through years of real life.

Here is a straightforward guide to the most common interior sheens — and where each one belongs in a Minnesota home.

Understanding the Sheen Scale

Paint sheen refers to how much light the dried surface reflects. From lowest to highest shine:

  • Flat / Matte — No shine. Hides imperfections best. Harder to clean.
  • Eggshell — A soft, low luster. The most popular choice for living spaces.
  • Satin — Slightly more reflective than eggshell. Wipes clean easily.
  • Semi-gloss — Noticeable shine. Very durable and moisture-resistant.
  • High-gloss — Mirror-like finish. Used sparingly on trim and accents.

The higher the sheen, the more durable and washable the surface — but the more it reveals flaws in the wall underneath. That is why prep work matters just as much as product selection.

Room-by-Room Recommendations

Living Room, Dining Room & Bedrooms

Best choice: Eggshell or matte. These spaces need a calm, even look without glare bouncing off every wall. Eggshell gives you a little more washability for high-traffic areas like hallways connected to the living room. Matte is ideal for adult bedrooms and formal dining rooms where walls are rarely touched.

Hallways & Stairwells

Best choice: Eggshell or satin. These are the most abused walls in any home — backpacks, laundry baskets, and shoulder bumps take a toll. Satin holds up to repeated wiping without showing every mark.

Kitchen & Bathroom

Best choice: Satin or semi-gloss. Steam, grease, and splashes demand a finish you can wipe down without damaging the paint. In bathrooms especially, Minnesota homes run humid all winter with windows closed — a higher sheen resists moisture and mildew better than flat paint ever will.

We typically use satin on kitchen and bathroom walls and semi-gloss on trim, cabinets, and doors in those rooms.

Trim, Doors & Baseboards

Best choice: Semi-gloss. Trim takes the most contact of any painted surface in your home. Semi-gloss is tough enough to survive vacuum bumps and furniture nicks, and the slight shine creates a clean contrast against eggshell or satin walls.

Ceilings

Best choice: Flat. Ceilings rarely get touched, and flat paint hides the subtle texture differences that show up under overhead lighting. Using anything shinier on a ceiling creates an unwanted spotlight effect.

Kids' Rooms & Mudrooms

Best choice: Satin. When crayons, muddy boots, and sticky fingers are part of daily life, you want a finish that cleans up with a damp cloth. Satin strikes the right balance between durability and a comfortable, non-plastic look.

What Minnesota Homeowners Should Keep in Mind

Homes in Duluth and the Twin Ports face a few conditions that affect sheen choice:

  • Long heating seasons — Closed windows from October through April mean indoor humidity concentrates in kitchens and bathrooms. Do not use flat paint in moisture-heavy rooms.
  • Older plaster and drywall — Many Duluth homes have walls with decades of texture, patches, and uneven surfaces. Higher sheens on damaged walls highlight every imperfection. Flat or eggshell on walls, with careful prep, gives the best result.
  • Natural light variability — Overcast winters and bright summer afternoons change how sheen reads in the same room. Test your chosen finish on a large sample board and view it at different times of day before committing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using flat paint in bathrooms or kitchens — It absorbs moisture and stains instead of repelling them.
  • Matching wall sheen on trim — Trim and walls should be different sheens. The contrast defines the architecture of the room.
  • Going too glossy on large wall areas — Semi-gloss on every wall in a room creates a clinical, reflective look and shows every roller mark.
  • Skipping sample boards — Sheen looks different on a 2-inch chip than on an entire wall. Always test at full scale.

Our Go-To Combinations

For most Duluth-area interior projects, we recommend this pairing:

  • Walls: Eggshell (living spaces) or Satin (kitchens, baths, hallways)
  • Trim & doors: Semi-gloss
  • Ceilings: Flat

We use premium Sherwin-Williams lines like Duration Home and Emerald Interior because they deliver consistent sheen levels batch to batch — so touch-ups down the road actually match.
